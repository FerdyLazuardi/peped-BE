# Adult Learning Theory (Andragogy) — Knowles

This file answers questions about adult learning, andragogy, how I design for working professionals (not students), Knowles, designing for field officers, training adults, why corporate L&D differs from school education, pembelajaran orang dewasa.

## Quick answer

I lean on **Malcolm Knowles' Andragogy** — adults learn fundamentally differently from children, and that shapes everything from how I write objectives to how I script training videos for Amartha field officers.

## Knowles' 5 assumptions about adult learners

| Assumption | Implication for my design |
|---|---|
| **1. Self-concept** — adults are self-directed | I publish learning paths, not mandate them step-by-step. Rise 360 modules let learners skip content they already know. |
| **2. Experience** — adults bring prior knowledge | Every Amartha course starts with "what do you already do?" — Modal Cycle Zero simulations use FOs' real client conversations as scaffolding, not generic case studies. |
| **3. Readiness to learn** — adults learn what they need now | I never train ahead of need. Cycle Zero training fired right when the new product was about to launch — not 6 months early. |
| **4. Orientation to learning** — problem-centered, not subject-centered | Objectives are framed around "what will you DO differently?", never "what will you KNOW?". |
| **5. Motivation** — internal > external | I emphasize "this gets you better at your job" over "this is mandatory training." Anti-Harassment course reframed compliance as "trust and safety" instead of "policy adherence." |

## Why this matters for Amartha specifically

Field Officers and Business Partners are **time-poor adult learners** with deep field experience but uneven formal education. Generic corporate e-learning fails them. My design choices:

* **Voice + tone**: "Hai A-Team!" — peer voice, not lecturer voice.
* **Examples**: Always real Amartha personas, never generic.
* **Length**: 3-6 min videos, not 30-min webinars. Adults won't sit through filler.
* **Application immediately**: Post-video simulation, not 4 videos then a quiz.

## Beyond Knowles — what I add

Andragogy is 1980s. I supplement with:

* **Self-determination theory (Deci & Ryan)** — autonomy, competence, relatedness. Why I let learners choose modules where possible.
* **70-20-10 model** — only 10% of workplace learning is from formal courses. Pair courses with on-job practice + manager coaching wherever I can negotiate it.

## Pushback I make to stakeholders

When stakeholders push pedagogy-style (long lecture, rote memorization, mandatory completion gates), I push back: "These are adult professionals. Treat them like investigators of their own job, not students." Doesn't always win — but it shifts the conversation.
