# SAM (Successive Approximation Model) and How It Compares to ADDIE

This file answers questions about SAM, the Successive Approximation Model, and how it differs from ADDIE. Use when comparing instructional design methodologies.

## Quick answer

**SAM** is a rapid, iterative instructional-design framework — three phases (Preparation → Iterative Design → Iterative Development) with frequent prototyping and stakeholder feedback. **ADDIE** is the linear-by-default backbone (Analyze → Design → Develop → Implement → Evaluate). I use ADDIE for non-trivial projects with clear stakeholders, SAM for tight-timeline AI/internal/prototype work.

## ADDIE vs SAM — at a glance

| Dimension | ADDIE | SAM |
|---|---|---|
| Shape | 5 sequential phases | 3 iterative phases with prototyping loops |
| Pace | Slower, gate-driven | Faster, prototype-driven |
| Best for | Compliance, certification, large rollouts | AI tools, internal pilots, tight deadlines |
| Risk | Late discovery of design problems | Less rigorous documentation |
| My usage | Modal Cycle Zero, Training Client Protection, Anti Harassment | AI Learning Assistant, Askfer chatbot, internal prototypes |

## How I actually decide between them

* **Use ADDIE when** stakeholders need formal sign-off per phase, the audience is large (1,000+), regulatory/compliance scope is involved, or evaluation rigor (pre/post, N-Gain) is non-negotiable. The artifact-per-phase discipline is what produces the **N-Gain 44.63%** on Modal Cycle Zero and **N-Gain 44.64%** on Training Client Protection.
* **Use SAM when** the timeline is tight, the spec is fuzzy, or the audience is internal and tolerant of rapid iteration. AI projects fit this — by the time you've finished a full ADDIE Analyze phase, the LLM landscape has shifted under you.

## My pragmatic blend

I rarely run pure-SAM or pure-ADDIE. In practice:

* I keep ADDIE's **artifacts** (curriculum map, assessment blueprint, storyboard) as deliverables — even when running SAM cycles around them.
* I bring SAM's **iterative prototyping** into ADDIE's Develop phase, building one full module end-to-end before scaling out (vertical slices).
* Evaluate happens **mid-project** in both — I pilot one module to a sample group before producing the rest.

## Other frameworks I lean on

- **Bloom's Taxonomy** for cognitive-level alignment of objectives and assessments (see my dedicated note).
- **Kirkpatrick's four levels** for evaluation design — Reaction, Learning, Behavior, Results.
- **N-Gain** as the headline learning metric for paired pre/post designs.
