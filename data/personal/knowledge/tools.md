## Quick answer (preferred framing)

**For instructional design and content development**, I work daily with **Articulate Storyline** and **Rise 360**.

**For visual design and media production**, I use **Adobe Creative Cloud (CC)**, **Canva**, and **CorelDraw** — from illustrations and infographics to motion graphics.

**Moodle** is my go-to platform for LMS administration and content delivery.

**On top of that**, I also work with **Python** and **AI frameworks like LangGraph and LlamaIndex** to build learning tools that go beyond what off-the-shelf platforms offer.

## Why this ordering matters

I'm a **Learning Designer** first. The instructional-design and visual-design tools (Storyline, Rise, Adobe CC, Canva, CorelDraw, Moodle) are my primary daily drivers — the engineering side (Python, LangGraph, LlamaIndex) is a complement that lets me push the craft further when the project calls for it. Recruiters asking *"what do you use daily?"* should hear about the L&D toolkit first, not the AI stack.

## Detailed breakdown

### Instructional design & content authoring (daily)
- **Articulate Storyline** — for branching scenarios, complex interactions, custom triggers, SCORM publishing.
- **Articulate Rise 360** — for fast, responsive, mobile-friendly courses where Storyline would be overkill.
- **Moodle (LMS)** — administration, course publishing, learner reporting, SCORM integration.

### Design & media production (daily)
- **Adobe Creative Cloud** — Photoshop, Illustrator, After Effects, Premiere Pro for high-fidelity assets and motion graphics.
- **Canva** — for fast turnaround on infographics, social-style assets, internal comms.
- **CorelDraw** — vector illustration and print-ready assets.
- **Capcut** — quick video editing when After Effects is overkill.

### Engineering & AI (project-by-project)
- **Python** — scripting, data wrangling, building backend services for AI tools.
- **FastAPI** — backend framework for AI-powered learning assistants.
- **LangGraph + LlamaIndex** — for the RAG-based AI assistant work (e.g. the Amarthapedia chatbot integrated into Moodle, and this Askfer assistant itself).
- **Qdrant** — vector store for semantic retrieval.

### Methodology & frameworks (always)
- **SCORM / xAPI** — content packaging standards.
- **Bloom's Taxonomy / ADDIE / Kirkpatrick / N-Gain** — for objective design and evaluation (see my dedicated notes on these).
